# Practice Problems: Probability Models and Parameters

This set of practice problems is designed to reinforce your understanding of probability models, parameters, and statistical inference.

## Problems Overview

### Problem 1: From Data to Probability

* Dataset: 100 exam scores
* Tasks:

  1. Calculate sample mean and variance.
  2. Estimate probability that a randomly chosen student scored above 80 using a probability model.
  3. Explain why using a probability model is better than relative frequency.

### Problem 2: Law of Large Numbers

* Flip a fair coin:

  1. Simulate 10 flips and record relative frequency of heads.
  2. Simulate 10,000 flips and record relative frequency.
  3. Explain how the Law of Large Numbers is illustrated.

### Problem 3: Understanding Parameters

* Population mean μ = 7000 steps, variance σ² = 1,000² steps²
* Tasks:

  1. Calculate population standard deviation.
  2. Calculate standard error for sample size n = 50.
  3. Explain why standard error is smaller than population standard deviation.

### Problem 4: Normal Distribution Application

* Heights of adult men: μ = 175 cm, σ = 7 cm
* Tasks:

  1. Probability a man is taller than 185 cm.
  2. Height cutoff for tallest 5%.
  3. Probability that sample mean of n = 49 men exceeds 178 cm.

### Problem 5: Real-World Decision Making

* Scenario: Netflix users and season completion
* Tasks:

  1. Probability exactly 7 of 10 users finish the season (Binomial).
  2. Explain why Normal approximation could be used for 100 users.

## Tips for Students

* Always calculate mean and variance first.
* Use Z-scores for Normal probability calculations.
* Probability models allow predictions beyond your sample.
